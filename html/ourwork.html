Our Impact & Programmes

Zakwantu Projects & Services is committed to empowering students and communities through bursaries, career guidance, study and life coaching, and educational projects. Over the years, our programmes have supported graduates across various fields, facilitated hands-on training through lab assistant and vacation work initiatives, provided mentorship and resources, and organized face-to-face visits and fireside conversations to guide learners toward success. Our holistic approach ensures that beneficiaries are not only academically supported but also equipped for meaningful careers and personal growth.

Programme Impact: Graduates

Our beneficiaries have successfully completed studies in various fields:

Face-to-Face Visits

During May, we conducted visits at CPUT, UWC, NMU, and UJ to monitor academic progress, provide mentorship, and support financial and personal well-being.

Pilot Student Khazimla Gongqa

Khazimla is training to become a commercial pilot, facing academic, practical, and emotional challenges daily. Her journey demonstrates resilience, discipline, and determination, inspiring young girls in her community to dream beyond traditional expectations.

She has passed five of her eight Commercial Pilot License (CPL) exams with outstanding scores above 86%. Beyond technical skills, she is developing leadership, problem-solving, and decision-making abilities. Her story highlights the transformative power of mentorship and access to quality training.

Lab Assistant Programme Benedict Van Rooyen

Benedict participates in the Lab Assistant Programme at PathCare Life Lab in partnership with AECFT. He gains hands-on experience in health sciences, bridging theory and practice, and learning essential laboratory techniques, quality control, and professional skills.

The programme emphasizes communication, teamwork, and ethical practices. Benedict expresses gratitude for the opportunity and reports strong progress and enjoyment in the programme.

Vacation Work Jonita Johnson

Jonita, a second-year BCom Accounting student at Rhodes University, successfully completed vacation work at GBG. This experience provided practical exposure complementing her academic learning and strengthened her confidence, career readiness, and skills for professional development.

Career Development Programme for Grade 12s

We provided workshops, peer-led discussions, motivational talks, and one-on-one coaching for Grade 12 learners in Somerset East, Bedford, Adelaide, and Cookhouse. The programme helped learners prepare for post-school studies and careers, giving them the tools, confidence, and awareness to thrive.

Dignity Packs Provision

We distributed dignity packs and blankets to Grade 12 learners during the JENN winter camp. This initiative supported hygiene and comfort, alleviating stress during winter months, and was well-received by learners and teachers.

Fireside Conversations

Our bursary students led informal peer-driven sessions with Grade 12 learners, covering academic pressure, campus life adjustment, time & financial management, and building resilience. Principals praised the initiative and requested future expansion to Grades 11 and 12.
